What do I do outside of class?

One of my favorite things about Purdue is the myriad of organizations that it has. There is really something for everyone! I am personally a part of three different organizations: BoilerMake, the Undergraduate Student Board for Computer Science (USB), and LaunchPad.

I am currently the Director of BoilerMake, an organization that hosts Purdue's largest hackathon. Lasting 36 hours, we give about 500 students the opportunity to work on projects, network with sponsors and other hackers, and have a fun break from school.

USB is an organization that works to improve the CS department at Purdue through their number of initiatives. Formerly the Vice-President of USB, I have now shifted my focus towards leading the Humans of Purdue CS initiative, an initiative that hopes to emulate HoNY. In addition to that, I am also a member of the International Student Panel, an initiative geared towards helping international students with job prospects, and CS193, an initiative geared towards freshmen in CS/DS/AI to help them learn the some of the fundamentals of CS.

LaunchPad is an organization that pairs freshmen, mentees, in a CS related major with upperclassmen, mentors, in similar majors to work on creating a project of the mentee's choosing. Within LaunchPad, I am currently the Web Dev lead where I lead a group of 4 other LaunchPad organizers to create and maintain our website.

Outside of these organizations, I am also involved in TA-ing. I have currently TA-ed CS 38100 (The Introduction of the Analysis of Algorithms) and TA-ed and taught CS 19300 (Tools). I find it to be extremely rewarding and fun as I am able to give back to the CS community while helping students find their "aha!" moment.
